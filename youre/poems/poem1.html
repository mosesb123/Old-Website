Misfire of Justice

Ruthie Vogel

Oh say can you see That standing here, together, We are easier to kill? You shouldn’t have to. School should be a place of safety – Not a war zone. What so proudly we hailed As one of our rights Was meant only to protect From the threat of a government; It was not meant To murder children Through the perilous fight Seconds were hours As their classmates died around them And they were forced to wonder: What kind of country Do we live in? Our flag was still there But it had failed in its duty Where is Domestic Tranquility? Since when did the right to life Become less important Than that of brutality The land of the free Is filled with the grieving And the brave are not soldiers But children Forced to live, forever With tragedy
